HACK THE FOREST DATA

Finland has one of the best forest data resources in the world, but there is limited number of commercial solutions utilizing that data. Create an innovative solution by combining forest resource data with other open data sources. In this challenge, basic data sets are offered by Finnish Forest Centre, National Land Survey of Finland (MML), Natural Resources Institute Finland (LUKE) and Finnish Environment Institute (SYKE). You may utilize these in your solution, but you are not limited to those. End-users can be — but are not limited to — forest industry, forest owners, other forest stakeholders or citizens.

Solution domains we appreciate

  • Forestry and Forest Resource Data:
    • 1. The visualization and demonstration of the forest by using for instance LiDAR data,
    • 2. Enriching the open forest data with crowdsourcing technologies,
    • 3. Theme maps based on the end user preferences and points of interests,
    • 4. Felling potential evaluation for the forest/public roads. Evaluating also areas, where road network needs to be established/improved based on the felling potential,
    • 5. The simulation of the forest growth based on possible forest operations enabling also the simulation of the impacts in carbon sinks for young forests.
  • Tourism and Related Ecosystem Services: A mobile forest navigator providing the optimal route search in the forest. Enriched with the virtual reality elements.
  • Protection of Waters: GPS based electronic guide for the protection of waters, which provides a guidance for protecting the waters when planning forest operations. It helps to plan the protection of waters in advance and prior to any forestry operations for instance based on the terrain shapes.
  • Technologies suggested: Virtual Reality, Crowdsourcing, Mobile Application, Big Data and multiple data sources, IOT/Sensors.

EVENT MOBILITY HACK

Challenge

Create and bundle new technology and smart mobility services to make it easier for event attendees to reach events and move around the city during events and travel home happily. Selected event organizers and venues will be available for developers to test their solutions earliest in Q3-4/2018.

Solution examples

  • Before the event(-s): Solutions which utilize smart mobility services for event and city marketing purposes, such as attendee engagement and interaction
  • Before the event(-s): Solutions which that utilize smart mobility services to make actual trip to an event happen smoother
  • During the event(-s): Solutions which bundle other event and city services to movement around the city
  • During the event(-s): Solutions which enhance the event experience
  • For event management: Solutions which help event organizers manage added services to their events more easily and effectively
  • For event management: Smarter ticket systems, which help bundle services to events and drive audience interaction
  • After the event: Solutions which utilize smart mobility services for event long term audience engagement
  • After the event: Solutions which utilize smart mobility services to make trip back home smooth and highlight the positive event experience

Constraints

  • Platforms are preferred over event apps.
  • New standalone solutions without any connection to existing services are not preferred.

Background

This hack will be backed up by multiple Finnish cities and event organizers. More info about the challenge partners will be announced later.

ESA BIC SPACE HACK

Challenge

Space industry took us to the moon, flew the space shuttle 135 times, building the Space Launch System for NASA, to eventually take astronauts back to the International Space Station (ISS) and on to Mars. Space Technology comes in a multitude of forms: positioning and navigation to communications, robotics, nanosatellites, augmented reality, autonomous driving, self-navigating and self-flying devices, ships and vehicles, novel materials, artificial intelligence, innovations specifically for arctic conditions, gaming, data visualization.

Come up with ideas, visualizations and solutions related to marine economy, arctic conditions, air quality and forestry which utilize Copernicus and/or FMI data resources. When submitting to this challenge, you are allowed to send the Space Hack submission also to either “The Talsinki Hack/ Marine” or “Hack the Forest Data/Forestry”.

THE TALSINKI HACK: HACK THE COMMUTE AND CROSS-BORDER BUSINESS

Challenge

Create solutions for people commuting between Tallinn and Helsinki weekly. Come up with new innovative services and solutions that make the commute smoother, shorter and more comfortable. You can extend your imagination beyond commuters and think about how to make cross-border business more effective and comfortable for employees and employers working in these two cities.

Solution domains we appreciate

  • Commuter journey and customer experience: Make commuter experience as pleasant as you can, find points for friction and fix them in new innovative ways.
  • Make the commute (more) green: eg. infrastructure environmental monitoring.
  • Logistics and efficiency: Make your journey as less time and effort consuming as possible.
  • Build community: Think of ways to create and foster cross-border startup / business community.
  • Open new business in Estonia/Finland - how to lower barrier of start business in a new market.
  • Solutions could be eg. Apps for ferries, Recruitment apps, matchmaking apps, environmental awareness programs, community communication solutions, cross-border business acceleration, add-ons to map solutions, improvements to existing travel sites/solutions/apps, especially services that are missing from current commuter experience.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTION

Should you have any other query, ping us at info@streethack.org

1. Is this StreetHack event free of charge?

YES. TOTALLY. With specific conditions (described in this FAQ), we offer you free accommodation, free snacks, free drinks.

2. How can I submit my project to be considered for StreetHack ?

Register / Sign in at https://streethack_demo.streethack.org, apply to StreetHack Demo hackathon and submit your project with as much detail as you can. It’s that simple!

3. How/when can I be eligible for the prizes ?

Submit your project plan to StreetHack Demo hackathon page. Join us at the event. Showcase your skills and project to convince the judges your solution is the best!

4. Do I have to stay at the 48H hack venue for the whole duration?

No. But as a minimum, we expect you to register at the event on Saturday Morning, show your project to the judges on Saturday evening and join the Finals/Award ceremony on Sunday evening. But who would want to miss the spirit of the whole event? So be there, it will be AMAZING. StreetHack Demo hackathon will be held at Investopad, Gurugram.

5. How can I get a travel grant?

Sorry, For StreetHack Demo, we couldn’t arrange for travel grants this time.

6. Can I participate if I cannot write code?

Yes you can! But we definitely recommend you to team up with some people with coding skills. You can search for them at ultrahack.org and ask them to join your team.

7. Can I participate without a team or with a virtual team?

YES. We accept talented individuals to hack. We also accept individuals who have virtual team to back them up. Although we appreciate highly matchmaking before and at the venue.

8. How can I register my team?

Each team member should apply at https://streethack.org/streethack_demo . When you submit your Project, just tag your team members to the project so we know who are involved in which project. Keep the project information up-to-date.

9. Is there limit to number of team members?

YES. 4.

10. What kind of accommodation is available?

Sleeping is possible at the venue ( mattresses etc) in a quiet area.

11. Is there going to be food?

We will be serving free snacks and meals (breakfast, lunch, dinner) throughout the day. Also free coffee, tea and other refreshments will be available.

12. What about the food allergies or specific travel or sleeping arrangements?

In those cases, we'll tailor you something special! Please contact Mr Rajiv Jha rajiv [at] streethack.org {+91-9871381106}

13. What should I bring with me?

Bring your own laptop and other devices such as your phone, tablet, charger, adapters etc. If possible come-up with an internet device for your team, which can be used in emergency by you, as our servers will be handling large amount of traffic. Bring also your personal items and whatever you think you would need during your weekend.

14. How can I protect my idea, when everyone can see my project plan?

Of course there are limitations how to protect your idea when all ideas are visible. It is all about how you can manage to execute your idea. And remember - source code is not visible to all! Source code can be shown only to the judge. All IPR retains with you, and doesn’t transfer in this competition. However, challenge owners have the first buyer right for 3 months after the 48H event is finished.

15. Question about our IPR. Are we losing or is our employer company in risk of losing IPR or patents if we participate to Ultrahack India hackathon? What does the term “Right of First of Refusal” mean?

The ROFR only concerns the IPR created by the teams, not IPR owned or created by partners, organizers, etc. The team owns all IPR they have created, but if they want to sell this IPR to a third party, the track’s main partner may have the right to buy the IPR instead (becoming the buyer in the third party’s place). Generally, if a partner brings their own IPR to contestants, they are encouraged to do a separate agreement on use license of that particular IPR for the duration of the hackathon contest.

16. Can I start working on idea before ?

Yes. We believe in functional working solutions, and We also believe in True spirit of hackathon. So, You will be judged on the work done in 24 hour at hackathon. But, we encourage to prepare yourself before hackathon.
